## Front Garden Bike Store — Research (pre-purchase, Mar 2026)

### Why a bike store

- Mountain Buggy Duet V3 and bikes are taking up front-of-house space
- Front garden hedge (2.4m) provides natural screening — any unit under that height is visually hidden
- Planning requirement: front garden structures need Adur District Council permission; 133cm well under 2.5m threshold

### Other units considered

- Various metal and plastic bicycle stores compared on Shedstore, Asgard, Trimetals direct
- Key constraints: internal height must accommodate folded Duet V3 (89cm); width for two adult bikes + future kids' bikes/scooters
- Trimetals chosen for: UK manufacturing, 25-year guarantee, RHS endorsement, zero maintenance PVC-coated steel, anthracite colour suits Victorian terrace

## Lawn Edging Research

- Compared 4cm vs 6cm depth metal and plastic lawn edging
- Context: border between turf and flower bed on gently sloping sandy loam
- Metal (aluminium or steel): more durable, holds edge better on sandy soil that shifts
- Plastic: cheaper, lighter, easier to install, less durable long-term
- No confirmed purchase on record

## Garden Furniture Research

### Acacia vs Eucalyptus for coastal conditions (Shoreham)

| Property | Acacia | Eucalyptus |
|---|---|---|
| Durability | Very high — dense, hard grain | High — comparable |
| Maintenance | Annual oiling required | Annual oiling required |
| Weight | Heavy | Lighter |
| Sustainability | Variable (check FSC cert) | Better options available (FSC certified more common) |
| Coastal suitability | Excellent | Excellent |

**Habitat × Scion folding wooden chairs:** Checked suitability for year-round outdoor use in Shoreham. Folding wooden chairs in acacia: suitable if stored or covered in winter. Salt air accelerates wood degradation — oiling twice yearly in coastal location recommended.

## Soil Science Notes (sandy loam)

Sandy loam characteristics relevant to this garden:
- Drains quickly — waterlogging not a risk, but drought is
- Warms up faster in spring than clay — good for early planting
- Lower natural nutrient retention — benefits from regular compost addition
- Ideal for Mediterranean drought-tolerant plants (rosemary, lavender, sage)
- New turf needs intensive watering for establishment — the twice-daily requirement is genuine on this soil type, not conservative advice
