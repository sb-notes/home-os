# Steve Irwin — Garden

---

## Property & Garden Context

- **Property:** Victorian semi-detached terrace, 53 Victoria Road, Shoreham-by-Sea, West Sussex
- **Aspect:** WSW-facing — good afternoon and evening sun
- **Garden dimensions:** 15m long × 5.5m wide
- **Layout (front to back):** 4m sandstone patio → 6m lawn → rear patio + raised beds
- **Soil:** Sandy loam — drains quickly, dries fast in warm/breezy coastal conditions. Add grit to planting holes for drought-tolerant species; mulch well to retain moisture.
- **Topsoil used in beds:** Eurogreen 50/50 topsoil and compost mix
- **Climate context:** Shoreham coastal — mild winters, sea breezes, moderate rainfall. Tolerant of most evergreen Mediterranean plants. Wind can be a factor for tall/exposed plants.

## Plants & Shrubs

### Rosemary — 'Roman Beauty' (Salvia rosmarinus 'Roman Beauty')

- **Planted:** July 2025 (purchased Boma Garden Centre)
- **Position:** Mid-garden, best all-day sun
- **Character:** Compact mounding habit, violet-blue flowers, extended flowering season, very tidy. Not the sprawling type.
- **Care:** Full sun; well-drained soil (add grit if needed); drought-tolerant once established; light prune after flowering — do not cut into old wood.
- **Pairs well with:** Lavender, sage, drought-tolerant grasses — Mediterranean effect.
- **Status:** Established.

### Smoke Bush — Cotinus coggygria

- **Planted:** Summer 2025
- **Character:** Deciduous. Dramatic red/purple foliage in summer, smoky plume flowers. Goes completely bare in winter — normal, not dead.
- **Care (year 1):** Water during dry spells; mulch roots in autumn; avoid waterlogging. Hard prune in spring for larger leaves and stronger colour (sacrifices flowers).
- **Status:** Bare in winter; returning with spring growth.

### Peony

- **Moved to garden beds**
- **Needs:** Full sun (minimum 6 hours — WSW aspect adequate if not shaded). Does not like being disturbed once settled.
- **Care:** Plant crown no more than 2–3cm below soil surface (too deep = no flowers). Water well in first season. Top-dress with compost in autumn.
- **Status:** Settling. Leave undisturbed.

### Jasmine

- **Plan:** Train up fan trellis attached to fence
- **Trellis spec:** 120cm high, fan style (3 upright planks with cross bars)
- **Species to use:** Common jasmine (Jasminum officinale) for fragrance; or trachelospermum (star jasmine) for evergreen + fragrance — better for WSW coastal aspect as more wind-tolerant
- **Status:** Not yet planted.

### Spanish Bluebells (Hyacinthoides hispanica)

**Status: Active management programme required**

- Arrived with the property. Spreading significantly through lawn and beds (observed March 2026 — significant clumps through turf, particularly right-hand side near trellis fence).
- UK invasive non-native species — do NOT compost. Brown bin or black bin only.

**Lawn programme:**
- Let current season's flowers bloom and foliage die back naturally (6–8 weeks post-flowering = approx. June 2026)
- Begin regular robot mowing from June — repeated close mowing starves bulbs of energy over 2–3 seasons
- Expect meaningful reduction over 2–3 years of consistent mowing

**Bed programme:**
- After flowering dies back, dig out bulbs with hand fork to 15–20cm depth
- Sieve soil carefully to remove all offsets (small bulblets spread the problem)
- Check again in autumn for any regrowth
- Repeat annually — not a one-season job

**Note:** Pre-flower mowing only delays flowering ~1 week; does not kill bulbs already primed underground.

## Lawn

- **Size:** Approx. 6m × 5.5m
- **Turf laid:** Mid-July 2025
- **Base:** Sandy loam — critical to water deeply and frequently when establishing

### New turf care (key learnings for reference)

- Water twice daily (morning + evening) first week; deep soak to 10cm — non-negotiable on sandy loam
- Keep off for 2–3 weeks minimum
- First mow: ~3 weeks after laying, high blade setting
- Top-dress turf joints with sand/soil mix immediately after laying
- Fertilise lightly after 4–6 weeks

### Sprinkler

- **Purchased:** Oscillating sprinkler (eBay)
- **Why:** Rectangular spray pattern — matches 6m × 5.5m lawn footprint; avoids overspray onto patios and path. Impact sprinklers produce circular pattern — harder to control in this shape.
- **Tips:** Place parallel to lawn edge; adjust width and range carefully; water early morning or late evening to minimise evaporation.

### Robot mower

- **Purchased:** Bosch robot mower (compact, suits ~33m² lawn)
- **Why:** No petrol, no cord, minimal storage footprint
- **Models considered before purchase:** Husqvarna Aspire R4, Flymo EasiLife Go, Worx Landroid S300, LawnMaster L10
- **Winter care:** Store indoors — protect lithium battery from frost
- **Storage:** Keter City 113L Outdoor Storage Box (grey) — fits under windowsill (max 90cm H). External dims: 57.8cm W × 44cm D × 54.8cm H. Robot mower with handles folded confirmed to fit. 96% recycled material, weatherproof, zero maintenance.

### Lawn edging

- Researched 4cm vs 6cm metal/plastic edging for border between turf and flower bed on gently sloping sandy loam. No confirmed purchase recorded.

## Soil & Materials

### Treated timber in flower beds

- UC4 pressure-treated timber under flower beds: safe for ornamentals. Copper leaching is minimal and binds to soil.
- Lifespan in-ground: 10–15 years (quality UC4)
- **Avoid:** Old CCA-treated timber (pre-2004 — contains arsenic)
- **Tips:** Postsaver sleeve or sheet plastic barrier at soil line extends life. Oak or cedar = longevity without chemicals.

### Hardcore / rubble sourcing

- **Hove Recycling Centre** (Old Shoreham Road): disposal only. Monthly limit 6 small bags (~25kg each). Not a source.
- **Shoreham Recycling Centre** (West Sussex County Council): closer option. Check for materials scheme.
- **For sourcing:** Freecycle, Gumtree, local social media, leftover building materials from neighbours.

### Topsoil

- Eurogreen 50/50 topsoil + compost mix used in raised beds. Good all-purpose for this garden.

## Garden Furniture

- Researched **acacia** (Laura James) vs **eucalyptus** (Deuba/XXI) for outdoor durability in Shoreham coastal conditions
- Acacia: heavier, needs annual oiling, denser grain, handles wet well
- Eucalyptus: lighter, more sustainable sourcing options, similar durability, slightly less common in UK market
- Habitat × Scion folding wooden chairs: checked suitability for year-round outdoor use in Shoreham
