## Garden — Lawn
- Turf laid **mid-July 2025** on sandy loam

### New Turf Care (key learnings)
- Water twice daily (morning + evening) for first week; deep soak to 10cm — critical on sandy loam
- Keep off for 2–3 weeks minimum
- First mow after ~3 weeks at high blade setting
- Top-dress joints with sand/soil mix immediately after laying
- Fertilise lightly after 4–6 weeks
### Sprinkler
- Chose **oscillating sprinkler** (purchased on eBay)
- Reason: rectangular spray pattern suits 6m x 5.5m lawn; avoids overspray onto patios and path
- Impact sprinklers ruled out — circular pattern harder to control
- Tips: place parallel to lawn edges, set width/range precisely, water early morning or late evening

## Garden — Soil & Materials
### Treated Timber in Flower Beds
- UC4 pressure-treated timber under flower beds is safe for ornamentals — copper leaching is minimal and binds to soil
- Expected lifespan in-ground: 10–15 years (quality UC4)
- Avoid old CCA-treated timber (pre-2004, contains arsenic)
- Tips: use Postsaver sleeve or sheet plastic barrier at soil line; consider oak or cedar for longevity without chemicals
### Hardcore/Rubble for Beds

**Key learning:** Lems sizing is generally right (EU 48) but their last is too narrow/low-volume at the midfoot. All Lems shoes required a 6-month break-in to expand. Primal Zen insoles improved fit significantly.
### Research conclusion: move to Freet
- Freet's "5in1" last is more generous in midfoot width and vertical volume than Lems — better suited to 11.5cm-wide foot
- Freet EU 49 internal upper width ~118–120mm — foot sits entirely on sole with room to splay
- Feelgrounds Patrol Lite sole width 107–110mm at EU 48/49 — too narrow for 11.5cm foot in non-stretch vegan leather; ruled out
- **Recommended size for Freet: EU 49** (not 48) — needed for ~12mm length clearance and to avoid midfoot compression
