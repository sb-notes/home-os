## Auckland Suburb Shortlist

Research focused on North Shore Auckland. Ranked by overall fit:

- Direct beach access, manageable commute to CBD

- More affordable North Shore option
- Community feel, local pubs/cafés, less polished
- Good compromise between location and budget if stretching isn't possible

### 5. Titirangi

- Bush/coastal west Auckland, creative/arts community, very different vibe
- More Waitakere Ranges aesthetic than North Shore
- Great fit for Hannah's arts/culture values
- Less convenient for Ollie if commuting to Auckland CBD

### Also researched

- **Waitakere / Te Henga (Bethells Beach):** Surf beach, rural coastal, beautiful but higher cost (NZ$7,463–11,108/month) and limited amenities

- **Whangarei:** 90 min north. Cheaper (NZ$5,370–7,465/month). Relevant if extended family (potentially NZ family network from Te Henga to Whangarei) relocate.

## Hannah's Visa Pathway

Hannah is not a NZ citizen and will require a visa to live and work in NZ. Two most likely routes:

**Option A: Skilled Migrant Resident Visa (points-based)**
Hannah is an Italian, French, English and art teacher — language teaching is an identified skills shortage area in NZ. She may qualify on the points system as a skilled migrant. This is the cleanest long-term pathway to permanent residence and would allow her to work freely as self-employed.

**Option B: Partner visa via Ollie's NZ citizenship**
As Ollie's spouse, Hannah is eligible for a partner residence visa. This is straightforward in principle but requires documentation of the genuine relationship, and processing times can be significant.

**Both routes will need professional immigration advice.** Do not assume either is automatic or fast.

## Tax & Cross-Border Notes

- UK/NZ double taxation treaty applies — UK rental income declared in both jurisdictions, foreign tax credits claimed
- **NZ transitional residence exemption:** New NZ tax residents get a 48-month exemption on foreign income — including potential Rightbrain equity gains. This is a major planning lever for EMI option timing.
- **Important caveat on exemption eligibility:** Ollie worked in NZ for ~5 years (student part-time jobs) and then 2.5 years full-time as a taxpayer after university, before moving to Australia. This means he has *prior* NZ tax residency. The transitional residence exemption is designed for new NZ tax residents and may be affected by this prior residency history. However, Ollie has been a non-tax-resident of NZ for ~11+ years (Australia + UK), which may be sufficient to qualify as a "new" tax resident again on return. **This must be specifically confirmed by a cross-border specialist — do not assume the exemption applies.** This is a potentially six-figure planning question (Rightbrain EMI gains).
- CGT on UK investment property sales: 24% (higher rate residential, confirmed Spring Statement 2026 — no changes)
- CGT crypto losses (>£19k carried forward) can offset gains — coordinate with Maslins
- **Cross-border accountant:** Engage PWC or Deloitte NZ (first 30 mins often free) before disposal sequence begins. Do not leave this until the year of departure.
- April 2027 UK property income rate change (42%) = hard planning deadline for property disposal
