---

## Completed Trips

### Bordeaux, France — August 2025

**Character:** UNESCO-listed city. Former English colony (Eleanor of Aquitaine → Henry II). Major 18th-century Atlantic trade hub including slave trade. Nazi occupation and French resistance history. Now: progressive politics, community urban farms, radical bookshops, strong food market culture. Excellent fit for anti-corporate slow travel approach.

**Context:** Extreme heatwave (40°C+) during trip — orange/red alert across southwest France. Hannah has endometriosis — heat management was a key concern. Considered cancelling; investigated Nationwide FlexPlus cover — heatwave not covered unless government travel ban declared (it wasn't). Stayed and managed carefully.

**Practical notes:**
- 7-day TBM public transport pass (trams, buses, river shuttle) — buy at tram stop (physical ticket). TBM app useful for timetables and live info.
- Botanic Garden area recommended for cooler green-space walks during heatwave
- Points of interest: Basilique Saint-Michel (organ concerts), Bassins de Lumières (immersive art), Porte Cailhau, Place de la Bourse, Café Brazza area for food

### Isle of Wight — First Family Holiday with Twins

**Status:** Under consideration. No booking made.

**Why it works:**
- Ferry access from nearby Portsmouth / Lymington / Southampton — no flying, short journey from Shoreham
- Beaches suitable for toddlers (sheltered bays, sandy beaches, calm for small children)
- Slow pace, no big-city overwhelm
- Good indie accommodation options (quirky Booking.com, Airbnb cottages)

**Considerations for twin toddlers:**
- Keep the trip short (3–4 nights maximum first time)
- Prioritise proximity to beach and low stimulus over cultural content
- Avoid peak summer school holiday crowds if possible — May half-term or early September window better
- Car hire or own car on island is practical with car seat logistics

**Areas to consider:** Bembridge (quiet, sailing, beach), Freshwater/Totland Bay (west, dramatic cliffs and calmer beaches), Ventnor (south, warmer microclimate, indie shops), Yarmouth (ferry town, small, walkable).

## Travel Philosophy Notes

- "Travelling is the best way to understand that your way is not the only way."
- Preference for places with political complexity and honest history — not sanitised tourism
- Architecture, public space, local food markets, and community art signal a city's soul
- Sustainability matters: public transport over car hire, local guesthouses over hotel chains, farmer's markets over supermarkets
- At this life stage: toddlers travel better with routine anchors — one base, day trips rather than multiple moves. Slow is better.

---

## Booking Principles

- **Accommodation:** Airbnb (quirky homes, local hosts) or Booking.com (filter: high guest review, non-chain, unique properties). Avoid all major hotel chains.
- **Food:** Research local market days before booking accommodation. Plan meals around markets, not restaurants.
- **Transport:** Public transport first. Car hire only if rural area with no PT coverage and children's kit makes it impractical otherwise.
- **Insurance:** Nationwide FlexPlus includes travel insurance for the family. Note: heatwaves and climate-related events are generally NOT covered unless government travel ban is in place.
