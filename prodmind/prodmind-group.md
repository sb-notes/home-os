# ProdMind — Group Design & Method

---

## Purpose

ProdMind is a free, member-run peer support group for senior product people — Heads of Product, Group Product Leads, and those on the CPO track. It exists to give product leaders a confidential monthly space to voice real challenges, get honest input, and build genuine peer relationships with people who understand the work.

**No coaches. No sales. No corporate framing. Run by the community, for the community.**

---

## Group Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Target size | Up to 15 members |
| Session attendance | Aim for 5–7 per call |
| Frequency | Monthly — second Friday of the month |
| Session time | 3:00–4:30pm or 3:30–5:00pm UK (work hours) |
| Format | Online video call, ~90 minutes |
| Cost | Free |
| Method | Roundtable peer coaching (adapted) |
| Week to avoid | Third week of each month (scheduling conflicts identified) |

---

## Target Member Profile

- Heads of Product, Group Product Leads, CPO-track leaders
- Senior individual contributors who operate at leadership level
- People who want honest peer input, not coaching or networking theatre
- No junior PMs — this is a peer group, not a mentoring programme

---

## Session Format — Roundtable Peer Coaching Method

Based on the roundtable peer coaching method (see Notion link). Key principles:

- One person brings a real challenge per session — the "focus person"
- Others ask clarifying questions before offering perspective (no rushing to solutions)
- Facilitator keeps time and holds space, not the agenda
- Confidentiality is the foundation — what's said in the room stays there
- No hierarchy in the room — co-founders participate as peers, not leaders

**Adapted elements for ProdMind:**
- Start simple with core roundtable method
- Bring in additional formats (case studies, lightning rounds, hot seats) as group matures and members request variety
- 90-minute session allows one or two focus topics with time for open discussion

**Facilitation:**
- Rotate facilitation among willing members over time
- Co-founders facilitate early sessions while establishing group norms
- Goal: group self-facilitates within 3–6 months

---

## Learning from Forever Dads (parallel model)

ProdMind draws on lessons from the Forever Dads adoptive dads support group:

- Even 2 people showing up is enough to run a session — don't wait for critical mass
- 15 members is the target for reliably getting 5–7 per session
- The group format builds trust faster than networking events
- Community-run = longer-lived and more honest than professionally facilitated groups
