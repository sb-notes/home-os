# Aunty Jen — Quick Meals (Children's Reference)

Fast, tired-parent meals for the children. These are the everyday staples — not recipe-book material but essential to have at your fingertips at 5:30pm when energy is low. Fetch on demand.

Children: two kids, approximately 26 months old at time of writing.

---

## Pasta with Butter & Parmesan

**Time:** 10 minutes  
**Equipment:** Hob (Cookmaster)

1. Boil salted water. Cook small pasta (fusilli, penne, conchiglie) per packet — usually 8–10 minutes for children's portions.
2. Drain. Immediately toss with a generous knob of butter.
3. Grate parmesan over. Toss again.
4. Cool briefly before serving.

**Variations:** Add a spoon of cream cheese, a little mild pesto, or a drizzle of good olive oil.

## Scrambled Eggs on Toast

**Time:** 5 minutes  
**Equipment:** Hob (Cookmaster), Scoville pan

1. Crack 2–3 eggs into a bowl. Beat lightly with a splash of milk.
2. Melt butter in a non-stick pan on low heat.
3. Add eggs. Stir gently and constantly — do not rush.
4. Remove from heat just before fully set (residual heat finishes them).
5. Serve on buttered toast, cut into strips.

**Notes:** Low and slow is the secret. Takes 4 minutes properly done.

## Porridge

**Time:** 5 minutes  
**Equipment:** Hob or Haden microwave

**Hob method:**
1. 50g rolled oats + 300ml whole milk (or milk/water mix) per child.
2. Bring to simmer stirring constantly, 4–5 minutes until thick and creamy.
3. Serve with a drizzle of honey or mashed banana.

**Microwave method:**
1. Oats + milk in a deep bowl (it bubbles up).
2. 2 minutes on high, stir. 1 more minute. Stir again.
3. Leave 1 minute before serving — it's very hot.

## Baked Beans on Toast

**Time:** 5 minutes  
**Equipment:** Haden microwave or Cookmaster hob

1. Heat beans in a small pan or microwave (2 minutes, stir halfway).
2. Toast and butter thick bread.
3. Pour beans over. Cut toast into soldiers.

**Notes:** Add a pinch of smoked paprika to the beans if you have it — lifts them considerably.

## Banana Pancakes (Nick's)

**Time:** 10 minutes  
**Equipment:** Hob (Cookmaster), Scoville frying pan

*See full recipe in `auntyjen-recipes.md`.* Quick summary:
1. Mash 2 bananas + 2 eggs. Heat coconut oil in pan on medium-low.
2. Drop spoonfuls into pan. 2 minutes each side.
3. Serve immediately.

**Notes:** Children love these. Naturally sweet, no added sugar.

## Yoghurt, Banana & Oats (No-Cook)

**Time:** 2 minutes  
**Equipment:** None

1. Full-fat Greek or natural yoghurt in a bowl.
2. Slice banana over the top.
3. Sprinkle with rolled oats or granola.
4. Optional: drizzle of honey or a few blueberries.

**Notes:** Almost instant. Protein, fat, carbs — genuinely balanced for a tired evening.

## Cheese & Veggie Omelette

**Time:** 8 minutes  
**Equipment:** Hob (Cookmaster), Scoville frying pan

1. Whisk 2–3 eggs with a little salt. Grate some mild cheddar.
2. Dice whatever soft veg you have: courgette, peas from frozen, spinach.
3. If using hard veg, soften in the pan first (2 minutes in butter).
4. Pour in eggs. Leave to set on low heat, lifting edges as needed.
5. Scatter cheese and veg over half. Fold over.
6. Slide onto plate and cut into strips.

## Avocado on Toast

**Time:** 3 minutes  
**Equipment:** Toaster, Cookmaster (optional for poached egg add-on)

1. Toast and butter bread.
2. Halve and scoop avocado. Mash with a pinch of salt and a squeeze of lemon.
3. Spread on toast. Cut into pieces.

**Optional add-on:** A poached or fried egg on top for extra protein.

## Rice with Peas & Mild Curry

**Time:** 15 minutes  
**Equipment:** Instant Pot (pressure cook) or Cookmaster hob

**Instant Pot method:**
1. 150g basmati rice, rinse well. Add 180ml cold water, pinch of salt, ½ tsp mild curry powder.
2. Pressure cook 3 minutes. Natural release 10 minutes.
3. Stir in a handful of frozen peas (residual heat warms them).

**Hob method:**
1. Same ingredients. Bring to boil, cover, reduce to lowest heat, 12 minutes. Remove from heat 5 minutes.

**Notes:** The rice absorbs the mild spice well. Not too spicy — these are small children.

## Cream Cheese Pasta

**Time:** 10 minutes  
**Equipment:** Hob (Cookmaster)

1. Cook small pasta as usual. Reserve a small cup of pasta water before draining.
2. Drain pasta. Return to pan off heat.
3. Add 2 tbsp full-fat cream cheese + splash of pasta water. Stir until silky.
4. Optional: a little smoked salmon, peas, or mild cheddar folded in.
5. Cool briefly. Serve.

---

## Soup from a Carton / Tin (Boosted)

**Time:** 5 minutes  
**Equipment:** Haden microwave or Cookmaster hob

1. Heat a good carton soup (tomato, butternut squash, pea — mild flavours work best).
2. Boost it: stir in a little cream, a knob of butter, or a spoon of Greek yoghurt for richness.
3. Serve with buttered bread for dunking.

**Notes:** Not cooking, but not nothing. Carton soups are often underseasoned — taste and adjust.

## Notes

- All these meals can be on the table in under 15 minutes.
- The children are ~26 months — finger food, strips, and bite-sized pieces are preferred.
- Mild flavours throughout; spice can be added separately for adults.
- These meals do not belong in the family recipe book — they're operational quick-reference only.
