## Ras al Hanout Pork Belly — Dual Lentil Braise

**Recommended by:** Ollie (developed in conversation with Aunty Jen, 5 Apr 2026)  
**Source:** Original — developed in kitchen  
**Appliance:** Instant Pot Pro Crisp 8L — Pressure Cook (High) + Air Crisp finish  
**Serves:** 4 (portioned as 2 meals of 2 servings each)

**The joint:**
- 700g pork belly joint (use plain, unmarinated — see heat warning below)

**Spice layer:**
- Ras al hanout, dusted over pork before searing

**Red lentil base (bottom layer — dissolves to sauce under pressure):**
- 150g red split lentils, rinsed
- 1 tin (400g) chopped tomatoes
- 1 medium carrot, diced
- 300ml water
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp cinnamon
- ½ tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp maple syrup (or coconut blossom syrup)
- Salt to taste

**Puy lentil layer (middle — holds texture):**
- 150g green puy lentils, rinsed (scattered over red lentil base — do not stir)

**Method:**
1. Sauté (High): splash of olive oil, sear pork belly skin-side down 3–4 mins, flip 2 mins each side. Remove.
2. Dust pork with ras al hanout.
3. Add red lentil base ingredients to pot. Stir.
4. Scatter puy lentils over — do not stir. Layering matters.
5. Nestle pork on top, skin-side up, proud of liquid.
6. Pressure Cook High, 45 minutes.
7. Natural release 15 mins, then quick release.
8. Lift pork carefully — very tender. Red lentils dissolve into thick dal-like sauce; puy lentils hold shape within it.
9. If too loose: Sauté lid-off 5 mins.
10. Air Crisp lid at 200°C for 10 mins to crisp the skin. Or oven grill, high, 8–10 mins.

**Optional sides:** Courgette (Air Crisp 200°C 12 mins), pearled spelt (boiled 20 mins), squeeze of lemon, fresh parsley or coriander.

**Storage & reheating:**
- Keep pork skin away from lentils in storage — skin submerged overnight will not crisp back.
- Cool fast: leave lids off 20–30 mins before sealing.
- Fridge (up to 3 days): lentils — Sauté or saucepan with splash of water 5–8 mins. Pork — pat skin dry, Ninja Combi Air Fry 200°C 10–12 mins.
- Frozen: defrost overnight in fridge. Same reheat. Do not reheat pork from frozen in air fryer.

**Notes:** Ras al hanout suits this braise better than Egyptian five spice — more complexity (rose, cardamom). Dual lentil approach is essential — red lentils become the sauce body; puy lentils provide texture contrast. Do not stir or combine the layers.

⚠️ **Heat warning:** If using a pre-rubbed joint (e.g. Lidl habanero/chilli pork belly), the combined heat of the pre-rub plus ras al hanout (which also contains chilli) is too much for the household. Use plain, unmarinated pork belly and control the spice blend from scratch.

## Sheila's Christmas Cake

**Recommended by:** Ollie (family recipe — Sheila / Grandma Clayton)
**Source:** Family heirloom — Sheila's version of Grandma Clayton's recipe, via the CAKES folder
**Folder:** CAKES

**Ingredients:**
- 250g butter (or margarine such as Olivani)
- 150g white sugar
- 4 eggs
- 1 glass rum
- 150g figs, chopped
- 900g dried fruit
- 250g plain white flour
- ¾ tsp nutmeg
- ¾ tsp allspice
- ½ tsp baking powder
- ½ tsp curry powder
- Pinch salt
- 100g whole peeled almonds (optional, for top)

**Method:**
1. Cream butter and sugar. Add eggs and mix well to combine.
2. Add all dried fruit and rum.
3. Add all dry ingredients and mix well.
4. Place in lined tin. Decorate with almonds if using. Bake at 150°C for 2 hours. Best made at least 1 month ahead — it ages well.
5. Line base of tin with cooking paper or greaseproof paper, increase height of sides. Place a piece of plain brown paper over cake after 1 hour (prevents top drying out, cracking or crisping). Bake until skewer comes out clean.
6. Leave in cooling oven in tin until cold.

*Eat and be happy.* — Sheila

**Notes:** The curry powder is the secret ingredient. Identical recipe to Grandma Clayton's Christmas Cake already in the book — this is Sheila's copy of the same family recipe.

## Ollie's Water Kefir

**Recommended by:** Ollie  
**Source:** Household staple

**Ingredients (per batch):**
- 720ml filtered water (NOT tap — chlorine damages grains)
- 3 tbsp raw soft brown sugar, organic
- 1 tbsp black molasses
- 2 slices fresh lemon
- 2 slices fresh ginger
- 1–2 tbsp dried fruit, sulphite-free (apricots, cherries, sultanas, prunes)

**Method:**
1. Stir sugar and molasses into filtered water.
2. Add water kefir grains, lemon and ginger slices.
3. Cover with cheese cloth and rubber band.
4. Leave on bench 2 days.
5. Drain through plastic sieve into jars; add dried fruit.
6. Start next batch; refrigerate previous batch next day.

**Key tips:**
- Plastic sieve only — metal harms grains.
- First 2 batches are warm-ups; expect sweeter result.
- Batch 3+ will taste properly fermented.
- Hibernation: fridge up to 1 week; freezer up to 1 year.
- Wash grains in water occasionally.

## Marzena's Dark Danish Rye Bread (Gluten Free)

**Recommended by:** Marzena (Copenhagen)  
**Source:** Friend — transcribed from handwritten Danish original (Rugbrød)

**Timings:** Mix in morning, bake in evening — or mix in evening, bake in morning. (1 DC = 100ml in original)

**Note:** Called "rye bread" but contains no rye — completely gluten free, moist and dense.

**Ingredients:**
- 800ml water
- 100ml rice sourdough starter (see below)
- 50ml olive oil
- 20g psyllium husks
- 3 tsp sea salt
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- 170g whole linseed
- 170g whole buckwheat kernels
- 200g rice flour (or safflower flour)
- 100g buckwheat flour
- 150g sunflower seeds
- 1 tbsp cane sugar *(optional)*
- Wheat bran *(optional, to taste)*

**Method:**
1. Soak buckwheat kernels, linseed and sunflower seeds in the 800ml water for 3 hours.
2. Fold in all remaining ingredients with hands, spoon, or mixer.
3. Pour into lined bread mould.
4. Cover with plastic film; bench rest 8 hours.
5. Bake 200°C for 1 hour 10 minutes.
6. Leave to cool in oven with door closed.
7. Remove from mould; cool fully before slicing.

**Rice Sourdough Starter:**
1. Equal parts rice flour and water in sterilised jar; cover with cheese cloth.
2. After 24h (should bubble slightly): add 240ml water + 120g rice flour.
3. Repeat daily for 4 days.
4. Transfer to 1L screw-top jar; feed weekly (or replenish when baking).

## Janet's Date & Cacao Balls

**Recommended by:** Janet  
**Source:** Friend

**Note:** Source Google Doc was titled "Janet's orange cake" — appears to be a wrong title in the original Drive folder. Recipe confirmed as date and cacao balls.

**Ingredients:**
- 500g dried dates
- 4 tbsp raw cacao
- 2 tbsp desiccated coconut + more for rolling
- 240g (approx 2 cups) jumbo oats
- ~50ml water

**Method:**
1. Soak dates in lukewarm water 2–3 mins; remove stones by hand.
2. Blend in two batches: 250g dates, 1 tbsp coconut, 2 tbsp cacao, 1 cup oats, ~25ml water — coarse paste.
3. Combine both batches; knead 30 seconds.
4. Adjust: too wet → add oats; too dry → add water.
5. Form into balls; roll in desiccated coconut.
6. Fridge 1–2 days to firm up; freezer for longer storage.

**Notes:** Naturally sweetened, no refined sugar. Kid-friendly. Good as a snack or lunchbox item.

## Jake's 50/50 Sourdough (2 loaves)

**Recommended by:** Jake  
**Source:** Friend

**Timings:** Mix starter morning; bake following morning.

**Starter feed:**
- 150ml water + 150g starter + 100g wholemeal + 100g white bread flour
- Leave 5 hours (doubles in size); remainder back in fridge

**Main dough:**
- 730–740ml room-temp water
- 20g salt
- 300g starter
- 500g wholemeal bread flour
- 350g white bread flour

**Method:**
1. Mix water, salt and starter to slurry; add flours; mix to dough.
2. Autolyse 1 hour covered.
3. Stretch and fold: once at 1 hour; then 3–4 more times at 30–40 min intervals.
4. Refrigerate overnight.
5. Split into 2 proving baskets; room temp 1 hour; fridge 1 hour.
6. Heat oven and Dutch oven to maximum during proving.
7. Tip dough onto floured surface; lift into pot; lid on; bake 55–60 mins.
8. Remove from pot; cool on rack; bake second loaf.
9. Cool fully before slicing.

**Notes:** No kneading — stretch and fold only. Fridge storage post-bake aids digestion.

## Roland's Focaccia

**Recommended by:** Roland (New Zealand)  
**Source:** Friend

**Ingredients:**
- 574g spelt flour 630 (or strong white)
- 64g wholemeal spelt flour
- 510ml water
- 32ml olive oil
- 11–15g salt
- 9.6g fresh yeast
- Sun-dried or fresh tomatoes; fresh rosemary; whole garlic; fleur de sel

**Method:**
1. Combine all ingredients; knead to window test (~25°C dough temp).
2. Mature 3 hours at 20°C; stretch and fold at 30, 60, 90, 120 mins.
3. Transfer to parchment-lined tray (or IKEA casserole dish lined with parchment).
4. Press tomato pieces in with all ten fingers; spread dough evenly.
5. Rest covered 30 mins; add herbs; press in again; rest 30 more mins.
6. Bake 250°C on baking stone, no steam, 20 minutes.
7. Sprinkle fleur de sel.

**Notes:** Roland's preferred combination: rosemary and whole garlic. IKEA casserole dish works well for easy removal.

## Waitrose Roast Chicken with Celery & Beans

**Recommended by:** Waitrose magazine, January 2023  
**Source:** Published recipe. Hannah's favourite.

**Serves:** 4 | **Prep:** 10 mins + marinating | **Cook:** 50 mins

**Ingredients:**
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 175ml white wine
- 3 garlic cloves, crushed
- 1 leek, trimmed and thinly sliced
- 1 tbsp clear honey
- 8 British chicken thighs, trimmed of excess skin
- 6 sprigs thyme
- 2 celery hearts, stalks separated and cut into 5cm lengths
- 2 × 400g tins cannellini beans, rinsed and drained

**Method:**
1. Mix oil, 2 tbsp wine, garlic, leek and honey in a large bowl or bag. Add chicken, thyme and celery. Season and toss to coat. Marinate at least 20 minutes, ideally 1–2 hours in the fridge.
2. Preheat oven to 200°C. Pour remaining wine into a large roasting tin. Add everything from the marinade, turning chicken skin-side up. Roast 25 minutes.
3. Baste chicken and celery in the juices; roast a further 20 minutes.
4. Baste again, then stir cannellini beans into the tin. Roast a final 5 minutes until chicken is fully cooked.
5. Rest 5 minutes before serving.

**Serve with:** Mashed potato and/or steamed greens.

**Notes:** Gluten free. Freezes well up to 2 months. Use celery hearts — not celery sticks; hearts are sweeter and hold texture. Cannellini beans go in at the very end only — they'll go mushy earlier. The white wine base makes the pan sauce; don't skip it. Small 18.7cl bottles work if you don't want to open a full bottle, or freeze leftover wine in ice cube trays.

## Roland's Zucchini Feta Balls

**Recommended by:** Roland (New Zealand)
**Source:** Friend
**Folder:** AIRFRYER

**Ingredients:**
- 1 large zucchini — grate half coarsely, the other half finely
- 1 tsp salt
- 150g flour
- 100g panko breadcrumbs (plus more for coating)
- 2 eggs
- 200g feta cheese, crumbled
- 1 onion, finely chopped
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- Fresh mint and parsley, chopped, to taste
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Oil for brushing or rolling

**Method:**
1. Place grated zucchini in a sieve, sprinkle with 1 tsp salt, and leave 10–15 minutes to draw out moisture.
2. Squeeze out as much liquid as possible using a clean kitchen towel or your hands.
3. In a large bowl, mix zucchini with flour, panko, eggs, feta, onion, garlic, herbs, salt and pepper.
4. Leave mixture to rest a few minutes — zucchini releases more moisture and helps bind. If still too wet to form balls, add more flour or panko.
5. Shape mixture into small balls.
6. Lightly roll each ball in oil, then coat in additional panko breadcrumbs.
7. Place in Ninja Combi air fryer basket — do not overcrowd.
8. Cook at 180°C for 15 minutes.
9. Switch to Max Crisp / highest heat for 3–5 minutes to brown and crisp. Watch closely.

**Notes:** Serve hot — great as a snack, starter, or with a yogurt dipping sauce.

## Lyn's Crispy Sesame Tofu & Broccoli

**Recommended by:** Lyn (Mum)
**Source:** Family / adapted from Delish
**Folder:** AIRFRYER

**Ingredients:**
- 400g extra-firm tofu
- 2 small heads of broccoli, cut into florets
- 2 scallions (spring onions)
- 1 piece fresh ginger (approx 2.5cm), peeled
- 2 garlic cloves, peeled
- 180ml low-sodium vegetable broth
- 60ml reduced-sodium soy sauce (Lyn's note: try less — ¼ cup, not ½)
- 60ml pure maple syrup
- 2 tbsp chili crisp (2 shakes)
- 2 tbsp unseasoned rice vinegar
- 2 tbsp toasted sesame oil
- 1 tbsp + 75g cornstarch, divided
- 4 tbsp neutral oil, divided
- Toasted sesame seeds and steamed white rice, to serve

**Method:**
1. Press tofu: place on 3 layers of paper towel or clean tea towel, cover with more towels, place a heavy can or skillet on top. Press 30–45 minutes, replacing towels as they soak. (Can be done ahead — leave pressed tofu pieces in oil, salt and cornstarch.)
2. Preheat oven to 220°C. Cut broccoli into florets. Thinly slice scallions, separating green and white parts. Grate ginger and garlic into a bowl. Add white scallion parts, broth, soy sauce, maple syrup, chili crisp, vinegar, sesame oil and 1 tbsp cornstarch. Whisk to combine.
3. Tear tofu into rough 2.5cm pieces. Toss with ½ tsp salt and 1 tbsp neutral oil. Sprinkle with remaining 75g cornstarch and toss to coat. Arrange on parchment-lined baking sheet, drizzle with 2 tbsp neutral oil.
4. Bake tofu, turning halfway, until light brown and crisp — about 25 minutes.
5. When tofu has 12 minutes remaining: heat 1 tbsp neutral oil in large skillet or wok on medium-high. Add broccoli, season with salt, toss to coat. Cook until starting to char, 4–5 minutes. (Lyn's note: now does this in Ninja Combi air fryer at 200°C for 15 minutes.) Transfer broccoli to a plate.
6. Reduce to medium heat. Whisk sauce and pour into skillet. Simmer, stirring, until slightly thickened — about 4 minutes.
7. Add broccoli and tofu, toss to coat in sauce. Top with green scallion parts and sesame seeds. Serve over rice.

**Notes:** Do not add extra salt — the soy sauce carries enough. Lyn now does the broccoli in the air fryer at 200°C for 15 minutes rather than the stovetop char method.

## Papa's Classic Prawn Cocktail

**Recommended by:** Papa (Alan)
**Source:** Family / BBC Good Food
**Folder:** SEAFOOD

**Ingredients:**
- 400g cooked Atlantic shell-on prawns
- 4 Little Gem lettuces, trimmed
- 5 heaped tbsp mayonnaise
- 5 tbsp tomato chutney (or 4 tbsp tomato sauce)
- 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard (or 2 tsp creamed horseradish, or wasabi — use 1.5× the amount if using wasabi)
- Tiny splash of Tabasco
- Squeeze of lemon juice
- Paprika, for dusting
- 4 tsp snipped chives

**Method:**
1. Peel all but 4 prawns (reserve these for the tops). Break lettuces into individual leaves and divide between four small glass bowls. Sprinkle prawns over lettuce. Season with black pepper.
2. Mix mayonnaise, tomato chutney, Worcestershire sauce, mustard/horseradish and Tabasco. Season with lemon juice, salt and pepper.
3. Spoon sauce sparingly over the prawns. Dust with paprika, sprinkle with chives. Top with reserved prawns.
4. Serve immediately. Delicious with brown bread.

**Notes:** Shell-on prawns are superior for flavour — worth the peeling effort. Save shells for stock. Classic 1970s starter, beloved of Papa. Papa's substitutions: Dijon instead of horseradish; tomato sauce instead of chutney — both confirmed to work.

## Papa's Marinated Raw Fish Salad (Pacific Style)

**Recommended by:** Papa (Alan)
**Source:** Family / Ngati Porou Seafood
**Folder:** SEAFOOD

**Ingredients:**
- 300g fresh trevally fillets (or other firm white fish)
- Juice of 3–6 lemons or limes
- 1 tin (400g) lite coconut cream (use full-fat for a richer dish)
- ½ bunch spring onions
- Handful of coriander leaves, chopped (optional)
- 4 vine-ripened tomatoes, diced
- ½ cucumber, diced
- Salt and pepper to taste

**Method:**
1. Cut fish into small cubes roughly 1–1.5cm.
2. Place in a bowl with enough lemon or lime juice to cover. Mix well.
3. Leave in the fridge 1–2 hours for slightly firmer texture, or 3–5 hours for the fish to cook through.
4. Drain off citrus juice (optional — Papa keeps his in for tartness).
5. Add coconut cream.
6. Add spring onion, coriander, tomatoes and cucumber. Season with salt and pepper.

**Notes:** A Pacific Island classic — the acid from the citrus "cooks" the fish. Trevally is Papa's fish of choice; any firm white fish works. Keep the juice in if you like it tart.

## Alan's Quorn Moussaka

**Recommended by:** Alan (Grandad)
**Source:** Family / adapted from Easy Peasy Foodie
**Folder:** MAINS (shared)

**Serves:** 4 | **Time:** 45 minutes

**Ingredients:**
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 2 medium aubergines, sliced 5mm thick
- 1 small red onion, finely chopped
- 200g mushrooms, chopped
- 300g Quorn mince
- 1 vegetable stock cube
- Salt and pepper
- 400g tin chopped tomatoes
- 250g Greek yogurt
- 100g cheddar cheese, grated

**Method:**
1. Preheat oven to 180°C fan.
2. Fry aubergine slices in batches in oil in a large pan on high heat — brown both sides. Set aside.
3. Turn heat down. Fry onion in oil until lightly browned.
4. Turn up heat. Add mushrooms, fry until browned. Add Quorn and turn heat down.
5. Crumble over stock cube, season with salt and pepper, cook 1 minute. Add chopped tomatoes + ½ tin of water. Bring to the boil, then simmer 5 minutes.
6. Make topping: mix Greek yogurt and grated cheddar.
7. Layer in an oven dish: half the tomato mixture, half the aubergine, repeat, then top with cheesy yogurt.
8. Bake 20 minutes until bubbling.

**Variations (Alan's own):**
- Increase yogurt/cheese topping — especially if the dish is shallow.
- Add a double layer of thinly sliced (4mm) potatoes skin-on to the base, oiled and baked at 180°C for 25 minutes before adding the rest.
- Add a cup of coarsely chopped capsicum and/or celery to the mince mixture along with herbs/spices.

## Grandma Happy's Yummy Pudding

**Recommended by:** Lyn (Mum — Grandma Happy's recipe)
**Source:** Family heirloom (Grandma Happy)
**Folder:** DESSERTS

**Ingredients (single batch — double or triple freely):**
- 4–6 slices old bread

- 1 egg
- 110g butter (Lyn uses Olivani — "axle grease" per Bob Happy)
- 1 tbsp golden syrup
- 2 tbsp sugar

**Method:**

## Kiwi Waffles

**Recommended by:** Lyn (Mum)
**Source:** Family
**Folder:** DESSERTS

**Ingredients:**
- 90g rolled oats (1 cup)
- 5–6 Medjool dates (sugar substitute; use 2 tbsp sugar if no dates)
- 1 tbsp sugar (2 tbsp if no dates)
- ¼ tsp salt
- ½ tsp cinnamon
- 3 tsp baking powder
- 120g plain flour (1 cup)
- 60g wholemeal flour (½ cup)
- 375ml milk (1½ cups), plus more if needed
- 75g butter, melted (6 tbsp)
- 2 large eggs (or 3 small)

**Method:**
1. Place rolled oats and dates in a blender/Ninja cup and blitz until finely chopped.
2. Combine with all other dry ingredients in a large bowl.
3. Separate eggs. Beat whites. Blend whites with yolks, milk and melted butter.
4. Add wet ingredients to dry and mix with a hand beater. Add more milk if the mixture is too stiff — it should be slightly sloppy so it spreads over the hotplate.
5. Cook in a waffle iron until brown (Lyn's note: "brown means crisp — keep it in until it's just about to catch fire").
6. As you knock them out, place waffles on a cake tray so they remain crisp. Eat while still warm.

**Serve with:** Maple syrup. Stewed apple and yogurt optional but highly recommended.

**Notes:** Makes approximately 6 waffles. No waffle iron — consider the Ninja Combi on its appropriate setting, or cook as thick pancakes on the Cookmaster ceramic hob.

## Self-Crusting Coconut Pie

**Recommended by:** Lyn (Mum)
**Source:** Family / Google Drive (DESSERTS folder)
**Serves:** 6 | **Prep:** 10 minutes + cooling (2+ hours) | **Cook:** 1 hour

*There is a little bit of magic that happens when this pie is cooking — it separates into three layers: a crust bottom, a creamy custard centre, and a toasted coconut top. The original recipe uses whole eggs, but using egg yolks results in a creamier custard centre. Allow at least 2 hours to firm up after cooking; overnight in the fridge is even better.*

**Ingredients:**
- 6 egg yolks
- 100g (3.5oz) butter, melted
- 480ml (2 cups) milk
- 200g (1 cup) white sugar
- 90g (1 cup) desiccated coconut
- 60g (½ cup) plain flour
- 2 tsp vanilla extract

**Method:**
1. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F). Grease a 25cm (10 inch) pie dish.
2. Whisk all ingredients in a large bowl until well combined.
3. Pour into the prepared pie dish. Bake 1 hour, or until a skewer inserted in the centre comes out clean (the centre will still be a little jiggly). If the top is getting too brown, place a piece of tinfoil on top.
4. Allow to cool, then refrigerate at least 2 hours or overnight.

**Notes:** The pie self-separates into three distinct layers as it bakes — no pastry needed. Must be allowed to firm up properly before slicing; overnight is best. Egg yolks only (not whole eggs) give a creamier, less rubbery custard centre.

## Dan's Mushroom Quiche

> ⚠️ **Unverified recipe** — derived from memory clues, not yet confirmed by Dan. When Aunty Jen serves or references this recipe, she must note: *"This recipe is drawn from culinary knowledge and has not yet been confirmed by Dan. Please check with him before treating this as his recipe."*

**Recommended by:** Dan
**Source:** Derived from culinary knowledge — Ollie's memory clues (eggs, mushrooms, caramelised red onion, wholemeal pastry, tarragon)

**Pastry:**
- 170g wholemeal plain flour
- 85g cold unsalted butter, cubed
- Pinch of salt
- 2–3 tbsp cold water

**Filling:**
- 300g chestnut mushrooms, sliced
- 2 medium red onions, finely sliced
- 1 tbsp olive oil or butter
- 1 tsp fresh or dried tarragon (thyme works as a backup)
- 3 large eggs
- 150ml double cream
- 80g gruyère or mature cheddar, grated
- Salt and black pepper

**Method:**
1. Make the pastry: rub the butter into the flour and salt until it resembles breadcrumbs. Add cold water a tablespoon at a time and bring together into a dough. Wrap and chill for 30 minutes.
2. Preheat the oven to 180°C (fan 160°C). Roll the pastry out and line a 23cm tart tin. Prick the base, line with baking paper and baking beans, and blind bake for 15 minutes. Remove the paper and beans and bake a further 5 minutes until lightly golden.
3. Caramelise the onions: cook the sliced red onion in oil or butter on the lowest heat for 25–30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until deep golden and sweet. Do not rush this step.
4. Cook the mushrooms: turn up the heat and add the mushrooms to the pan. Cook until completely dry — wet mushrooms will make the base soggy. Add the tarragon, season well, and set aside.
5. Make the custard: whisk together the eggs and cream. Season generously.
6. Assemble: scatter the caramelised onion and mushroom mixture over the pastry base. Pour over the egg custard. Top with the grated cheese.
7. Bake at 180°C for 25–30 minutes until just set with a slight wobble in the centre.
8. Rest for 10 minutes before slicing. Serve warm or at room temperature.

**Notes:** The caramelised red onion is the key — do not rush this step. Tarragon is the classic mushroom herb for this quiche; thyme works as a backup. Use the same wholemeal pastry base as Lyn's Veggie Pie for consistency across the family recipe book. Mushrooms must be cooked until completely dry before going in — wet mushrooms will make the base soggy.
