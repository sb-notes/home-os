# Jamesbot — Job Search Playbook

*Reusable reference for next active job search. Synthesised from Product Sphere JSA workshops and Rightbrain negotiation experience.*

---

## 1. Before You Start — Clarity First

Define your next role in writing before doing anything else:
- What you love doing
- Must-haves and must-nots
- 1–3 goals for the role
- Stack-ranked list of attributes (compensation, scope, culture, mission, equity, location)

This becomes the strategy anchor for every decision — which roles to pursue, which to decline, how to position yourself.

---

## 2. Where to Put Your Energy

**High-ROI activities:**
- Inbound and warm paths (LinkedIn presence + network outreach) beat cold volume
- A target company list (~20 companies) — increases outreach quality and prevents scatter-gun effort
- Second touchpoints: apply + message 1–3 relevant people using a 3x3 follow-up cadence
- Treat the search like a product funnel: measure conversion and iterate (channel mix, messaging, role targeting)
- Speed wins in crowded funnels — saved searches and scheduled time blocks get you in early

**Low-ROI activities (deprioritise):**
- Cover letters — only worth it for perfect-match roles
- Portfolios — low signal, low usage in most hiring funnels
- ATS optimisation myths — optimise for human pattern-matching: role clarity, impact, obvious fit

---

## 3. The CV — James Gunaca / Product Sphere Standards

**Format:**
- Single-column, 1–2 pages max
- Last 6 years in full; earlier roles as one-liners
- Scannable in 10 seconds: tight professional summary at top

**Professional summary must include:**
- Scope (what level, what kind of product)
- Best outcomes (1–2 quantified wins)
- Domain fluency (B2B SaaS, AI, platform, etc.)
- Direction (what you want next)

**Bullet pattern — X by Y by Z:**
- Outcome → magnitude/timeframe → action
- First bullet per role = strongest measurable outcome
- Persuade through outcomes and scope; skills list is supporting detail, not the pitch

**Variants:**
- Maintain at least two positioning versions (e.g. platform/AI vs. broader product leadership)
- Fix any timeline inconsistencies — anything that invites doubt becomes an unnecessary interview thread

**Generate in .docx format, compatible with Google Drive.**

## 4. Applications — Workflow That Converts

- Track every role: source (cold/referral/inbound), JD copy-pasted (ads disappear), written answers saved
- Bookmark saved searches; create recurring calendar blocks for applications
- Salary fields: leave blank where possible; if forced, enter "negotiable"; if numbers-only, enter a minimal value; if range-only, pick higher than target
- Pair every cold application with outreach immediately after applying

- Use for second touchpoints (outreach + follow-up cadence) rather than passive presence

## 6. Interviews

### Elevator pitch — FIT framework
- **Frame** who you are (scope, domain, level)
- **Impact hooks** — 2–3 quantified outcomes
- **Turn** — why this role, this company, why now
- Time: 2–3 minutes max

### Story library
- Build ~20 stories (last 5 years) in STAR format with quantified results and clear "I" ownership
- This is a durable asset — use it for interviews AND for exec communication in-role
- Practice until stories are conversational, not recited

### Conciseness — Ollie's specific weakness to manage
- **Headline → two proof points → offer depth** ("Happy to go deeper on X or Y")
- Timebox: 2–3 min for pitch, 5–7 min for a project story
- Signpost transitions: "The trade-off was… The outcome was…"
- Brevity is a leadership signal — CPOs are concise

### Product sense questions
- Clarify → pick a segment → state structure → connect to North Star metric → keep scope narrow

### Asking good questions
- Ask about what success looks like in 90 days
- Ask about the biggest blocker the team is facing
- Ask about equity mechanics (vesting, refresh cadence, dilution expectations)

## 7. Negotiation — James Gunaca Principles

### The posture
- Negotiation signals seniority. Keep it business-first and depersonalised.
- "Would [Company] be able to..." not "Would you..."
- Don't accept on the call — ask for details in writing, take time

### Process
- Treat total comp as a system: base + bonus + equity + benefits + review cadence
- Bundle asks; cap rounds (aim for two)
- Use time as leverage: take the weekend, send questions in batches

### Equity — early-stage specifics
- Always verify: EMI vs non-EMI (£75k difference at £100M exit)
- HMRC valuation discount: 30–50% strike price reduction — always ask
- Check Companies House for cap table and early hire grant precedents (public filing)
- Push for milestone-based refresh grants, not just upfront allocation
- Get refresh commitment and milestones in writing at probation review
- Understand fully diluted maths and exercise window (90 days post-termination standard)

### Salary deflection
- Deflect early; let them anchor first
- Never accept verbally in the room

### Reference checks
- Do your own reference check on founders before accepting — worth 10X LinkedIn stalking
- Ask about: work-life balance, communication style, how they handled company failure/difficulty

## 8. Onboarding — 6 Pillars

1. Define success outcomes in writing (30/60/90 days)
2. Establish your work persona early
3. Set boundaries and norms explicitly
4. Build a people map (who matters, who influences, who blocks)
5. Go deep on product and customer journey before forming opinions
6. Learn mechanisms before you change them

**Practical behaviours:**
- Get expectations in writing
- Front-load 1:1s
- Avoid early commitments — capture requests and follow up once informed
- Make "informed recommendations" early, not "big decisions"

## 9. Career Roadmap — Ollie's Specific Path

| Horizon | Target | Evidence to build |
|---|---|---|
| 0–6 months (now) | Rightbrain: deliver, build trust, lock refresh milestones | Quick wins, metrics, customer insight |
| 6–18 months | Turn "founding product" into proof: PMF, hiring, operating mechanisms | Strategy doc, ICP playbook, team build, board-level comms |
| 24–36 months | Position for fractional CPO + NED (NZ context) | Advisory case studies, NZ network, outcomes narrative |

**CPO evidence bank — assets that make fractional and board work credible:**
- Strategic vision doc (crisp, board-ready)
- PMF/ICP validation playbook
- Hiring and team build record
- Metrics ownership track record
- Exec and board communication examples
- Customer insight cadence documentation
