## Strategic Review: HMO vs Whole-Unit Let

**Trigger for review (27 Mar 2026):** Difficulty finding replacement for Holly's room prompted this analysis.

- **Current HMO income:** £2,010/month (rooms + car space)
- **Whole-unit comparable:** £2,000–£2,200/month (estimate; target £2,100 with car space)
- **Assessment:** Broadly equal income but whole-unit = dramatically less management overhead
- **Recommendation:** Do NOT disrupt current tenancy. Natural trigger = when Amy or Lucie next depart. At that point, serve notice on remaining tenants and re-let as whole unit.
- **Benefits of whole-unit:** No flatmate churn, no SpareRoom ads, no individual references, no room disputes. If ≤2 unrelated adults or a couple: no HMO licence required.
- **Car space on whole-unit:** Include in headline rent — removes cash-in-hand complication, cleaner for tax.
