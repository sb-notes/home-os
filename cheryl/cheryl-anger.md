# Cheryl — Anger Log

Anger as signal, not character failure. Tracked for richness and pattern — incidence, triggers, resolution, intensity trend — not just frequency. The working frame from mid-April 2026: anger is a nervous-system signal the inner child has been pushed past margin, and is the trailing edge of the multigenerational chain Kiwifruit is the first in his family to break.

## Current state — mid-April 2026

**One week clear as of 18 April.** No incidents in the week leading up to 18 April. One near-incident the same week (Fox hit Kiwifruit on nose with nail clippers accidentally) contained calmly — Fox placed in cot, talked to, not an outburst. Kiwifruit feels *"really good about this progress."* This is the cleanest sustained stretch since the March–early April cluster.

## Trigger taxonomy

Primary triggers (from session 2 refinement + subsequent journals): Fox hurting Bunny (protectionist nerve); situations where children could have been hurt due to Kiwifruit's oversight (guilt-as-trigger); sense of right and wrong being violated; broken or damaged things (scarcity / conserve driver); accumulated fatigue + solo parenting + no release valve; not showering / self-care deficit correlates directly with severity.

Primary target through March–early April: Fox (~10–12 of 14 logged incidents). Bunny involved in 2 incidents (17 March). Almost exclusively when alone with the children — social awareness suppresses it with Carciofi or others present; but also more overwhelmed without support.

## Physical forms

Historical: throwing onto couch / bed; rough grabbing; squeezing (leg, head); one verbal (*"shut up"*); clenched teeth, strong voice. Present direction of travel: flush → pause → breath → heat evaporates → tenderness remains. Observer gap (anchor word Gina) doing real work. Intrusive thoughts about injuring disclosed honestly to `therapist-c` in Session 1 — clinically held as OCD-type ego-dystonic thoughts, not intentions.

## Trajectory

March frequency: ~every 3–4 days in active periods, 2–3 incidents per incident day. Session 2 (1 April): overwhelming anger every 5–7 days; manageable-but-present every 2–3 days. Early April 2–5: contained flushes and manageable-but-present incidents. 7 April: severe regression at rock bottom. 11 April: contained bedtime event, bridge moment. 12 April onward: equilibrium regained, no incidents for a week as of 18 April.

Working frame crystallising: anger is the trailing edge of a four-generation chain (great-grandmother's stepmother → grandmother → granddad + brothers → dad → Kiwifruit). Kiwifruit is the first in the family to seek therapy. Josh's reframe (Forever Dads, 8 April) landed: toddler parenting anger is near-universal but not discussed. The work is becoming *friendly* with anger — recognising it as signal rather than intruder.

## Resolution practices
