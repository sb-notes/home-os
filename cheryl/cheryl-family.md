# Cheryl — Family of Origin

Kiwifruit's NZ family: parents, sister, grandparents, intergenerational threads. Homesickness and NZ longing. Marriage and couple content belongs in `cheryl-hannah.md`. Parenting and adoption belong in Aloyse.

## Parents — Lynn and Dad

**Mum (Lynn).** Ultra-nice coping mechanism; occasionally overflows. Described in resourcing as one of the most consistent sources of security in Kiwifruit's life — *"unwavering, lifelong support."* Came from a large, hand-to-mouth, working-class family. "The nicest one" as self-protection from lifelong bullying by her sisters — now being exploited by her daughter (Sam) in adulthood. Engaged in her own therapy (disclosed on a phone call, 16 March); has spoken to Dad about it.

**Dad.** Anger through tools, traffic, outdated language toward Mum (*"bloody woman,"* *"Ah, bloody woman!"*). Zero-to-angry-with-no-warning pattern — family walked on eggshells, *"don't poke the bear"* dynamic. Educated, capable man who was made redundant (at around the time Kiwifruit was born or age ~6 — to clarify) and had to return to work at his father-in-law's instrument factory making nuts and bolts. A professional and personal humiliation. Then 28 years as a geologist at Winstone's, staying long after the redundancy-package window had widened. Gripped the safety of the known. Eventually took his package at retirement, long after the optimal moment.

Dad understood through the intergenerational trauma lens, not blame. *"1950s NZ culture that dismissed mental health as mumbo jumbo"* — unlikely to pursue therapy at his age. Mum reflected that Dad was never as involved in parenting as Kiwifruit is — a generational contrast. Dad's 80th birthday approaching in April 2026 in NZ; Kiwifruit will not be there. Compounding homesickness.

Dislocated shoulder incident from childhood (Kiwifruit running past, Dad grabbed arm) — reframed as a parent now: *"I wouldn't let it happen"* — suggests Dad was rougher than previously understood. Revisited tentatively 16 March.

And yet: resourcing entry says *"on his side; feels very well supported by her."* The sister relationship is real and valued alongside the difficulty. *"An incredible childhood friendship; still a world-spanning bond."* Hazel (Sam's daughter, 3.5yo) was difficult to like during the Feb–March visit — honestly acknowledged — and Kiwifruit simultaneously holds a strong intention to be a positive long-term influence and uncle. *A 20-year project, not a 4-day problem.*

## Paternal grandmother — the effective second mother

Lived 2km away; Kiwifruit spent early mornings there through to age 12. Strong, loving but harsh. Her trauma: mother died in a bike accident after an argument — *"never let the sun go down on an argument"*; her evil stepmother burned her books; survived the London Blitz. Her anger modelling is now clinically live — new realisation (7 April): Kiwifruit was directly exposed to her shouting at granddad when she needed him for something. Adds a direct experiential layer to the intergenerational map beyond the theoretical one — body exposure to anger from **two** caregivers (Dad + grandma) during formative years.

Her death in the 2016 car accident: Kiwifruit walked into the morgue with Dad and granddad; couldn't cry — felt stuck and choked; cried only twice (morgue and funeral); felt guilt and *"something's wrong."* This sits on the Worst Memories List as the car crash cluster — high disturbance (7+/10), multiple layers: helplessness, loss of grandmother as second mother, identifying the body.

## Intergenerational chain — as understood today

Great-grandmother's evil stepmother → grandmother (harsh + loving) → granddad + brothers (Dad sent to boarding school; older brothers cruel to him) → Dad (tools, traffic, *bloody woman*) → Kiwifruit.

The parallel maternal chain: Clydebank anger from Clayton → Mum's *"nicest one"* coping mechanism → Sam (same anger pattern as Kiwifruit) + Kiwifruit.

The scarcity chain (separate thread, live with `therapist-c`): both parents from hand-to-mouth working-class families. The land / bank-manager event installed *abundance is for others, not for us.* Dad's redundancy installed *professional identity is fragile. Hold what you have.* Dad's anger at tools and objects is the same programme expressed somatically: *we don't have margin for damage.* Kiwifruit has inherited both — the object-damage anger (car scratch, breaking things) and the abundance-resistance at Rightbrain.

Key recognition: *the cross-generational trauma can stop with this generation.*

## NZ — childhood setting

Homeschooled by Mum and Dad's hard work until age 12. Pristine West Auckland environment. Described in resourcing as *"one of the most amazing childhoods he has heard of."* Always supported in sports, activities, interests — translated directly into his *"I'll just go and do it"* attitude. Culturally *"1950s England"* — home-schooled in rural NZ; parents culturally Edwardian / 1950s England. Very little traditional masculinity modelled. Repeatedly asked *"Are you sure you're not gay?"* by parents, friends, girlfriends — sensitive men not culturally recognised in that setting. Dad's *"mumbo jumbo"* attitude to therapy; *"good girl"* joke to young Kiwifruit.

Happy family genealogy: originates as Happé from La Rochelle, France — likely French Huguenot weavers. Relocated to London East End (Whitechapel, Bethnal Green, Stepney) post-French Revolution ~1789. Criminal history: Thomas Happy transported for life; Robert Happy transported to Cairns (1812). DNA: 36% Northwestern European, strong Swedish, Irish from paternal grandmother Kathleen. Possible Jewish connections (recurring biblical names — Jacob, James, Abraham). Cousin David Happy shared the full family tree. Genealogy discovery February 2026 landed with emotional resonance — finally explained why French people claim Kiwifruit as one of theirs.

## Homesickness and NZ longing

Acute in the 6–7 April cluster. Dad's 80th missed. Has missed grandparents' funerals, grandparents' 80ths, Mum's birthdays. Mum's email wording: *"You've grown up on the other side of the world from us"* — Kiwifruit read this as acknowledgement of permanence and duration. 23 years away = now past the halfway point of his life. *"I would really like to be home; I've had enough."*

Significant reframe 16 March 2026 (pre-therapy): *"I'm on my way home. It's just a question of how long it takes."* Previously: pain from wanting to go home. Now: urgency replaced by direction; comfort in the journey rather than anguish at the distance. Confidence that in NZ the children will have grandma, granddad, auntie, cousin, extended family — *"a huge support network."*

Live clinical question for `therapist-c`: is the NZ plan a healthy future-orientation, or partly an escape narrative — a way of keeping abundance perpetually deferred? Both can be true simultaneously. 17 April update: Kiwifruit naming clearly why England is the right place *now* — for EMDR with `therapist-c`, for Rightbrain, for the children settling, for Carciofi's book, for the properties. No longer feels trapped; can see the logic and choose it.
