## The "Oh Ollie" thread — live with `therapist-c`

The pattern, as it has crystallised through April 2026: at peak depletion, Kiwifruit's regulation falters (strong voice, clenched teeth — not physical). Carciofi, who has been silently accumulating, then leaks one comment — *"Oh Ollie."* It lands on Kiwifruit as verdict / judgement at the exact moment of maximum depletion. Kiwifruit receives it as collapse and spirals into shame. Neither is wrong; the pattern is not working.

The clearest instance: 11 April, bedtime — Fox had taken Bunny's comforters and wound her up; Kiwifruit used strong voice with clenched teeth; Carciofi said *"Oh Ollie."* Deep shame collapse followed. Same evening: the bridge-moment expression, Carciofi disclosed she has been biting her tongue and had noted a pattern of Kiwifruit being harsh almost invariably with Fox specifically. Ended with mutual *"I love you."*

The 7 April variant: in the car on the way to the family visit, Carciofi said she was scared of Kiwifruit when he was getting Fox out of the car — *"I hope he's not going to throw him on his head."* Kiwifruit received this into deep self-loathing; she later acknowledged the accumulation of stressors and the exhaustion visible to her.

## What would actually help — named by Kiwifruit 11 April

Bedtime interventions Kiwifruit identified as helpful at his Cheryl session:

1. Carciofi physically intervenes — takes child, offers distraction, swaps them.
2. Carciofi says *"just step out"* and holds both children for a few minutes.
3. Carciofi brings Bunny over and gives Kiwifruit a cuddle.
4. ***"Daddy's sad — should we help Daddy?"*** — named by Kiwifruit as the most powerful option. Reframes the moment, models emotional literacy, brings Carciofi alongside rather than watching. Clinically significant.

Outstanding action: pre-agreed pressure-valve conversation with Carciofi when both are calm. Share intervention ideas including the *"Daddy's sad"* script.
