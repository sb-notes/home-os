## Therapist pipeline — condensed history

Five therapists approached in early March 2026. Shortlist converged on four after intro sessions: `therapist-c` at £70 (concession rate; somatic / yoga / shiatsu background; EMDR-integrated; collaborative); Tom Taylor at £90 (attachment-informed; strong personal connection; cost sticking point); Phil Mitchell at £60 (men's specialist; direct approach; Leeds-based, remote); Simon Arthur-Smith at £55 remote (local, Hove; pre-vetted by Carciofi; EMDR qualified).

Decision made mid-March: `therapist-c` selected. Rationale — somatic background a clinical advantage (runs in parallel with `somatic-therapist-1` fascia work), EMDR integrated not bolted on, collaborative approach suits analytical style, engaged with complexity rather than jumping to solutions. £70 sustainable for 3+ months; significantly cheaper than Tom; Simon available as budget fallback if cost becomes decisive.

ASGSF funding (Adoption and Special Guardianship Support Fund) researched 14 March 2026: does **not** directly fund adoptive parent 1:1 therapy (child-focused, £3k/child/year). However, under the Adoption and Children Act 2002, adoptive parents have a statutory right to a formal adoption support needs assessment from the placing authority (Adoption South East) within 3 years of the adoption order. That assessment can identify parental therapeutic needs; local authority has discretion to fund. Frame as parental capacity support, not personal therapy. **Action still outstanding — contact Adoption South East.**
