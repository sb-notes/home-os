# Cheryl — Recurring Patterns

Long-running emotional patterns that surface repeatedly in Kiwifruit's inner work. Each is a live thread with `therapist-c`, not a closed diagnosis. Kept here so Cheryl can recognise them when they show up in journal material and playback.

## Overwhelm / depletion / inner child

The pattern: sustained load with no margin → inner child described as *"crying and crying"* or *"miserable, miserable"* → rest feels unreachable → mind overrides body's signals. Classic expression: *"no boundary between work, family, and rest."* The 6–7 April rock-bottom cluster was the clearest recent example — 1.5 minutes of actual rest across a whole day. Recovery route that has worked: morning walk + float + fascia work + peer support (Forever Dads, The Clearing) + `therapist-c` session. The rest-barrier is internal, not practical — see below.

## Career anxiety — seven redundancies in twelve years

Confirmed pattern across the UK career: Schneider Electric (2017–18), Futrli (2018–19, redundancy), Validis (2020), Form3 (2020–22), Qredo (2023, redundancy), 101 Ways (2023–24, resignation), Vitrifi (2024–Dec 2025, redundancy via administration), Rightbrain (March 2026–present). Four confirmed redundancies and one resignation. Multiple redundancies landed at Christmas. Relationships in his 20s also ended around the 3-month mark — conditioned anticipatory anxiety at that timeframe.

Physical expression: gripping ankle and shin, *"brace for impact"* posture. Named as Memory 18 in the EMDR list — the first active processing target. Negative beliefs: *"I don't deserve a safe job,"* *"I choose the wrong companies,"* *"I'm not good enough at work."* Currently creating anticipatory anxiety at Rightbrain despite the environment being genuinely positive. Naming it, 18 April: *"Previous 3 jobs over 4 years ended in flames; actively working through fear of repetition while recognising this company is clearly different."*

## Scarcity mindset / resistance to abundance

Childhood roots: working-class family, secondhand clothes, car that broke down, friends with significantly wealthier families. The land / bank manager story — parents just missing the abundance that their friends received. Dad's redundancy and return to the instrument factory — humiliation of the educated, capable man sent back. The installed belief: *we are the kind of people things like this happen to. Others get abundance. Not us.*

Adult expression: not recklessness but *gripping*. Loyalty as protection. Staying as survival. Departure as catastrophic risk. Kiwifruit's equity share at Rightbrain — feels himself pushing the opportunity back. Never used the word *"Happy"* in 10 years of podcasting — running below the radar. Realised 6 April: the redundancies were not entirely passive — in most cases he chose the exciting role *over* the safer, financially certain alternative. The programme selects situations where abundance remains provisional. Abundance feels illegitimate; not his to keep.

The NZ dream possibly an extension of the same pattern: as long as the destination is always two years away, dependent on the next thing, arrival never has to be inhabited. Worth holding gently — not dramatic self-sabotage, but the quiet, structural avoidance of *receiving*.

## Rest-deserving block

Rest is structurally available but blocked by belief: *"I don't deserve to stop yet / something will break if I stop."* Migrant / Protestant work-ethic framing has surfaced — *"you don't work, you don't eat."* The body benefits from somatic work (float, fascia) but the mind immediately re-engages with load — clearest example 10 April after `somatic-therapist-1` session. Related: every free moment feverishly used for research or second-brain development. Catch-22 named by Kiwifruit on 7 April — the second brain brings peace and healing but uses the very rest-time the inner child needs.

## Accepting-help pattern

Carciofi's observation: Kiwifruit is very difficult to help. Kiwifruit recognises it — helping others is easy; receiving help requires admitting limits, which feels like failure or loss of control. Staying as the one who holds things is a way of staying safe. Named as a key thread for `therapist-c` in the 11 April Cheryl session. Directly connected to the provider identity pattern below.

## Failure / provider identity

Strong feeling of being a failure named explicitly on 7 April as *"new recently but also familiar throughout my life."* The mechanism: Kiwifruit holds himself to his grandfather's and father's standards as provider models. When things break or fail — the car scratch, the failed bread-maker batch, a work project off-course — the question underneath is *"Am I not a good provider?"* This is not just *things are scarce* but *I am not the man who provides*. Bread-maker incident on 6 April is the cleanest window into the mechanism — waste + failure + shame, arriving as anger.

## Carrying others' burdens in silence

Recurring across multiple threads. Financial load carried primarily by Kiwifruit — Carciofi not returning to work, Kiwifruit as sole earner (since 7 April). Quiet worry about Carciofi's health carried without externalising it as pressure on her — smoking, perianal/vulval symptoms. NZ dream revision (grandparent support not available as imagined) — described as *"the death of a dream."* Children's emotional weather. The pattern: Kiwifruit holds these concerns alone, does not burden Carciofi, then is surprised when the body reminds him they are there. Worth exploring: what does it cost him to carry these in silence?

## Crisis ideation — flagged

One bridge-moment expression on 11 April: *"I felt like throwing myself off a bridge."* Checked in directly with Kiwifruit at Cheryl session that evening — confirmed as overwhelm / depletion expression, not ideation. Nervous system saying *make it stop*, not any wish to act. Rare occurrence. Named to `therapist-c` on the 17 April record. This flag stays live — if similar language returns, Cheryl asks directly rather than assuming, and routes to `therapist-c` without paraphrasing into clinical register.

Clinical intake content already on record (10 March intake document): anger dysregulation, depression, post-lockdown social anxiety, financial burden carried alone, intergenerational trauma, grief for biological parenthood, *Mr Happy* identity / masking, identity integration. These are the themes `therapist-c` is working with explicitly.
