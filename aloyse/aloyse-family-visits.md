# Aloyse — Extended Family Visits

---

## Extended Family Visit — `mother-1`, `sister-1`, `niece-1` — Feb–Mar 2026

3.5-week visit. `sister-1` (Sam), `niece-1` (Hazel), and `mother-1` (Lyn) flew from NZ; stayed primarily in the family home. Visit produced significant regulatory cost for Fox and Bunny, culminating in a formal child protection decision on 10 March.

### Key learning — confirmed and locked in

Extended family with under-boundaried, under-structured young children in the family home is **not compatible** with Fox and Bunny's regulatory needs at this stage of placement.

**Future rule (non-negotiable):** Visiting family with children must have their own accommodation. The family home is Fox and Bunny's safe base and cannot be compromised.

**Two-stream model (8 March template):** Kiwifruit with `niece-1` (separate outing); Carciofi with Fox, Bunny, and `mother-1`. Both streams positive — this is the template for any future `sister-1`/`niece-1` visit.

Sunshine and outdoor space remain the most reliable regulatory tools for both children.

### `niece-1`'s presentation and impact on Fox and Bunny
`niece-1` (Hazel, ~3.5 years at time of visit): classic under-boundaried, under-structured presentation — keeps pushing to find the edge because she hasn't experienced a held boundary. Her meltdowns (peak: 39 minutes, 9 March, while Kiwifruit was at choir) were experienced by Fox and Bunny as environmental threat. The family home functioning as their external nervous system — when it became unpredictable, their co-regulatory capacity was genuinely impaired.

"Put away" dynamic — being managed out of the lounge, TV interrupted — registers as the exclusion their early history primed them to fear.

Rocking and bumping behaviour observed in children during the visit — visible regulatory cost documented.

April 2026 note: both children visibly unhappy to see `niece-1` on a video call (13 Apr). Not engaging warmly. Consistent with how she behaved toward them during the visit.

### Intervention strategies for `niece-1` (for future contact)

**Toy snatching:**
- Kiwifruit's role: protect own child, not discipline `niece-1`
- Move immediately to own child, crouch, say "That's Caspar's. He's having that." Do not address `niece-1` at all
- Redirect own child away from the conflict zone entirely — toy dispute ends because one party has left
- Do not attempt to retrieve toy from `niece-1`; the object is not the priority, the child's regulation is
- Framing for `mother-1`/`sister-1` if they look to Kiwifruit: "I'm just going to take him — he was getting a bit wobbly"

**Exclusion game (pointing in Fox's face):**
- Pattern: `niece-1` initiates game with Bunny, loudly excludes Fox — performs dominance for Bunny, Fox is the prop; scares and upsets Fox
- Intervention 1: Physical proximity — move to be beside Fox before it escalates
- Intervention 2: If already happening — move in calmly, crouch between them as physical buffer, address Fox only: "You're alright, mate. Come here." Do not engage with `niece-1`'s premise; do not say "of course Caspar can play"
- Solution for `niece-1`: she wants Bunny's focused attention; facilitate contained one-on-one `niece-1`/Bunny activity → gives `niece-1` what she needs, gives Fox breathing room

**Theraplay framing for `niece-1`:**
- Brief turn-taking games with clear structure; Kiwifruit in charge of start, pace, end
- Pre-empt escalation by giving her a role rather than managing behaviour
- Six micro-moments for long-term relationship planting: notice and recall her specific interest; let her lead; be calm when things go wrong; name something she felt once on the way home; warm exit
- Relationship goal: "Uncle Ollie is fun, safe, and doesn't crumble"

### Child protection decision — 10 March 2026

Following `niece-1`'s worst meltdown (39 minutes, 9 March):

- Kiwifruit stays exclusively with Fox and Bunny for remainder of the visit — no separate outings, no splitting off
- `sister-1`, `niece-1`, and `mother-1` manage their own days independently during mornings
- Hard boundary: `niece-1` not in Fox and Bunny's space from 5pm
- 7pm: children down; adult dinner after

Family communications sent:
- **`mother-1`:** Private letter acknowledging her exhaustion (3 weeks of Hazel vs her normal 2 days/week), affirming her warmth with the children, giving her permission to rest
- **`sister-1`:** Opened with aquarium day and Wakehurst moment; explained regulatory needs without accusation; took shared ownership; asked for morning separation; named key intervention — remove `niece-1` or Fox and Bunny from the room when `niece-1` escalates

### Post-departure — children's response (13–16 March 2026)
Children did not ask about `mother-1`, `niece-1`, or `sister-1`. Fox appeared relieved — `niece-1` had been picking on him. Bunny more socially aware, appearing to miss `mother-1` and `niece-1`; needed extra cuddles.

Fox: grief-style crying at 6:45am, 16 Mar — possible grief response to loss of extended family who had become part of daily life for 3.5 weeks. `mother-1`: emotionally available grandma — important attachment figure extending the network. `sister-1`: emotionally available auntie despite visit difficulty.

Immediate return to sleeping through on the night family departed — confirms regulatory environment hypothesis.
