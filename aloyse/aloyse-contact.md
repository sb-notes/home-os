# Aloyse — Birth Family & Foster Carer Contact

All contact with birth family and foster carers. KiT framework, letterbox process, and life story material gathered through contact. Adoption SW: `adopter-sw-1` (Kat Clavell-Bate). Children's SW: `children-sw-1` (Michelle Earwaker).

## Foster Carers — `foster-carer-1` and `foster-carer-2`

### Contact framework
Twice-yearly visits recommended by `adopter-sw-1`. Rationale: post-contact reorientation toward primary carers is consistently positive — children churn up during and immediately after, then reorient warmly to Kiwifruit and Carciofi. This pattern supports continued contact being manageable and beneficial.

### Visit — 11 April 2026 (first in-home visit since Day 4 of placement)
Visit went well overall. Foster carers deferred to Kiwifruit and Carciofi on food portions and other decisions — clear parental hierarchy maintained. `foster-carer-1` and `foster-carer-2` allowed Kiwifruit and Carciofi to portion out Easter eggs and hide the rest; threw ball with children outside; no takeover of parenting duties.

Children's reaction: initially lukewarm, then openly happy. Both children visibly familiar with foster carers at a pre-cognitive, somatic level — especially Fox. Bunny verbalising the continuity; Fox less explicit but clearly affected somatically ("living with dementia" quality — feeling is present even without explicit episodic memory).

Children physically closed the front door on foster carers independently, then looked at Kiwifruit as if to say "lunch" — strong primary attachment signal.

Post-visit: Fox winding up, wriggly, dysregulated at bedtime. Bunny clingy, saying "Nana cramps." Both napped and settled eventually. Consistent with expected post-contact destabilisation — clinically normal.

Kiwifruit's clear realisation (12 Apr): the trigger for his anger and distress on 11 Apr was the foster carer visit. Both children spent extended time on foster carers' laps; attachment appeared to temporarily switch back. Both parents felt displaced. This is expected; the warm reorientation to primary carers afterward confirms the process is working.

### Earlier visit — Day 4 of placement (September 2024)
First in-home visit. No detailed record in current state files — referenced as the only prior visit before 11 Apr 2026.

## Birth Father — `birth-father-1`

### Profile
Train driver and artist. Warm, considerate, generous in sharing. Not contesting — explicitly chose not to contest because he wanted Fox and Bunny to have a stable home, rooted in his own father's strong moral influence. Came across with clarity and dignity throughout the April 2026 call.

### Video call — 17 April 2026
Attendees: `birth-father-1`, `children-sw-1`, Kiwifruit, Carciofi. Duration approximately 30–45 minutes. No uninvited third parties (unlike the `birth-mother-1` meeting). Described as "surprisingly heartwarming and positive."

### `birth-father-1`'s family background (life story material confirmed on call)

**Art and creativity:** Fox's deliberate, thoughtful mark-making and Bunny's bold, fast, expressive style are both confirmed to run in `birth-father-1`'s family. He is an artist as well as a train driver. His mother was very creative throughout his childhood (costumes, furnishings, crafts). His paternal grandfather did ornate ironwork in Brighton. Art appears multigenerational on this side.

**Build and physique:** `birth-father-1`'s father (paternal grandfather of Fox and Bunny) was from Eastbourne — one of five brothers and one sister. Tall and stocky build; described as where the family's large frame comes from. This contextualises both children's physical build and Fox's 99th percentile measurements.

**`birth-father-1`'s father (paternal grandfather of Fox and Bunny):** Served in the Navy, travelled the world, returned to Eastbourne. Community-minded (local gardening group). Strict but caring and loving; prioritised providing. Divorced from `birth-father-1`'s mother when `birth-father-1` was approximately 4 but remained a strong moral presence. `birth-father-1` credited his father's influence as a key reason he chose not to contest.

**`birth-father-1`'s mother:** Based in Brighton. Very creative. Has an alcohol addiction which continues; does not have active relationships with `birth-father-1`'s other children (minimal contact via phone). Fundamentally loving and caring, generous by nature — alongside the challenges.

**`birth-father-1`'s reasoning for placement:** Explicitly named wanting Fox and Bunny to have a stable, emotionally stable home as his core reason — something he knew he could not provide. Described with clarity and dignity.

### Half-siblings — `birth-sibling-1` and `birth-sibling-2`

**`birth-sibling-1`:** Turning 14 on 23 April 2026. Very mature from a young age. Has anxiety; `birth-father-1` noted "we're all somewhere on" the spectrum. Strong-willed. Recreates experiences from his day in the evenings. Loves being in the kitchen. Remarkable memory. Deep sensitivity to animals and others' suffering. Exploratory in art and creative making. Fox's exploratory, curious energy mirrors `birth-sibling-1`'s closely.

**`birth-sibling-2`:** Age 10. Strong-willed and precise — very detailed in artwork (attends a printing group on Mondays; her paintings noticeably more meticulous than `birth-sibling-1`'s despite being 4 years younger). Very motherly and compassionate — likely to become a nurse or similar. Was also a twin — her twin did not survive (bleed in utero). `birth-father-1` described `birth-sibling-2` as having a strong felt sense of connection related to this. Her precision, empathy, and motherliness echo Bunny's character closely.

**Parallel with Fox and Bunny:** `birth-father-1` found the personalities Kiwifruit and Carciofi described deeply familiar. Strong-willed nature described as a family non-negotiable. Bunny's empathy independently validated by `birth-father-1` as characteristic of his family line — mirrors `birth-sibling-2`'s character closely.

### `birth-father-1`'s early memories of Fox and Bunny
Met children approximately 4 times in total, all in Bromley. First meeting when children were approximately 9–10 months old.

First meeting: Bunny was upset and cautious (`birth-father-1` has a deep voice and large presence which overwhelmed her). Fox was very bouncy, strong in the legs, reaching for different things, very smiley and happy.

Later meetings: Bunny became progressively more relaxed. On one visit Fox had a cold and was subdued.

Final visit: `birth-father-1` brought his own father (Fox and Bunny's paternal grandfather) to the last meeting — wanted him to have photos and the lived experience to share with Fox and Bunny if they ask. Children were tiny in grandfather's hands. `birth-father-1` has photos from that final visit and childhood photos of himself to locate and share for memory books. Offered freely on the call.

## Birth Mother — `birth-mother-1`

### Profile
Contesting the adoption (as of April 2026) — applying under s.47 Adoption and Children Act 2002. Bar is high; placement order already in place; permission rarely granted at this stage. `adopter-sw-1`: "not cause for significant concern, standard contested process."

Positive qualities (`birth-father-1`'s perspective, shared with care): infectious energy, enthusiasm, and happiness. When "on form," described as a delight. `birth-father-1` hopes Fox and Bunny inherit `birth-mother-1`'s confidence and "get up and go." This perspective provides positive identity material for the children that `birth-mother-1` has not yet been able to provide herself.

`birth-mother-1` is a twin — her twin sister is Keeley. Both were early movers; enrolled in dance from a young age (ballroom, Latin, freestyle); both took exams. `birth-mother-1` continued dancing until her teens — described as "a very accomplished dancer." Clinically significant life story material: the children's love of music and movement is multigenerational on both sides.

**Reframe from `foster-carer-1`, 15 Apr prep call:** Fox and Bunny are "lucky" that `birth-mother-1` is contesting, because Kiwifruit and Carciofi can genuinely say when the time is right: "Your tummy mummy really wanted you — she just wasn't able to look after you." This gives the children a narrative of being wanted, not just removed. Comparable families where birth mums did not contest lack this narrative.
