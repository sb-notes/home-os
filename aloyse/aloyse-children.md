## Fox (Caspar)

### Attachment
Ambivalent-to-secure trajectory — on track at six months post-placement. Primary attachment to Kiwifruit; ambivalent pattern historically (tests Kiwifruit particularly). Mid-April 2026: strong positive shift — forehead-pressing, lap-climbing, extended cuddles are the longest and most sustained Fox has offered. Kiwifruit describes mutual bonding during lullaby sessions. Three consecutive nights without wake-ups confirmed mid-April.

Attachment-gut axis confirmed: when Kiwifruit is absent from a familiar context (first Horsham playgroup without him, A&E day, extended family visits), Fox's gut motility suppresses and sleep disrupts. Stress-mediated vagal gut stasis — monitor and raise with `therapist-c`.

Ambivalent attachment behaviour cycle (named March 2026): Kiwifruit going to work = loss trigger → Carciofi as secondary carer → avoidant coping activates → naughty/dangerous behaviour (dysregulation discharge + "are you still here?" test) → desperate clinginess once test passed. The naughty and the clingy are the same emotion — two ends of the same terrified loop. Pattern expected to shorten as Kiwifruit's working rhythm becomes established and Fox's nervous system registers "he goes but he comes back."

### In-the-moment intervention framework (Fox — Carciofi as primary carer)
- Don't match his energy: lower voice, slow movement
- Name the need not the behaviour: "You need Mummy. Here she is."
- Physical removal for danger: scoop not drag; outward-facing hold if in full push-away; "I've got you"
- Do not turn away, blank him, or use time-out — social exclusion confirms abandonment for a child in avoidant-ambivalent loop
- Time-in not time-out: if removal needed, Carciofi goes with him
- After every dysregulation: deliberate micro-moment of warmth — hand on back, "you alright mate", offer food/drink
- Check-in rhythm when calm: "There he is. I see you." — regular micro-deposits of attunement

### Handling Bunny wanting to be carried simultaneously
Sequence: Bunny first (60–90 seconds, full warmth), then Fox. If Fox doing something dangerous in that window: low flat voice without breaking contact with Bunny — "I see you, Cas. I'm just with Phoenix." After Bunny settled: crouch to Fox's level. Do not attempt both simultaneously — for Fox, being held alongside Bunny while dysregulated increases dysregulation.
